## 2.1. Measurement Principle

The 2D Video Extensometer is an optical non-contact deformation
measurement device. It captures sequential images of the specimen
surface through a camera and analyzes the displacement changes of
feature points in the images before and after deformation using Digital
Image Correlation (DIC) technology, thereby obtaining the displacement
change values of the specimen.

<img src="../../../../assets/products/video-extensometer/image3.png"
style="width:3.94861in;height:2.04792in" />

Figure 1 Schematic diagram of measurement principle

The main components of the video extensometer measurement system are
shown in Figure 1. The industrial camera completes image acquisition of
the specimen with the cooperation of the light source and computer. Four
standard feature points arranged on the specimen serve as the features
to be matched and tracked by the video extensometer.

## 2.2. Application Scenarios

1)  Metal material testing: Tensile, compression and bending tests for
    measuring elastic modulus, Poisson's ratio and yield strength

2)  Polymer materials: Large deformation testing of rubber and plastic
    (deformation up to more than 350%)

3)  Composite materials: Performance evaluation of anisotropic materials
    such as carbon fiber and glass fiber

4)  Microelectronics and thin films: Micro deformation measurement of
    chips and thin film materials

5)  Biomedical materials: Mechanical property testing of tissue
    engineering scaffolds and biomedical elastomers

## 2.4. Measurement Process

### 1) Set up the measurement system environment

1.  Install the video extensometer, camera driver and data acquisition
    > card software.

2.  Install fixed devices (portable tripod/rotating support/mobile
    > support), video extensometer and light source.

### 2) Pre-measurement preparation

Adjust the object distance and angle of the video extensometer to place
the system in a reasonable position and perform calibration. Arrange
feature points (standard marking points/hand-drawn marking points) on
the surface of the specimen to be tested.

### 3) Start measurement

Collect images in real time and calculate the deformation and strain of
feature points, and input the data into the testing machine system
simultaneously.

### 4) Result output

After the test, the deformation and strain data will be displayed on the
testing machine interface. The measurement results will be plotted as
line charts together with the testing machine's load, crosshead
displacement, stress, etc. At the same time, result parameters can be
set to obtain Young's modulus, Poisson's ratio, Rp0.2, tensile strength,
N value, R value, elongation after fracture, etc.

# 6. EQUIPMENT MAINTENANCE AND CAUTIONS

## 6.1. Daily Maintenance

### Pre-use inspection

5.  Appearance inspection: Confirm the equipment has no collision
    > deformation, the cover plate is undamaged, and the connecting
    > wires are not broken.

6.  System connection: Check whether the connection between the camera
    > main unit and the computer is firm and whether the power adapter
    > is working normally.

7.  Environmental conditions: Ensure the working environment temperature
    > is 5℃-40℃, relative humidity ≤70%, without direct strong light and
    > severe vibration.

8.  Equipment preparation: Take out the equipment and check for dust on
    > the surface of the front cover plate (air blower is recommended
    > for cleaning).

### Post-use cleaning

1.  Cover plate cleaning:

> • First gently blow off surface dust with an air blower to avoid
> scratching the cover plate by direct wiping.
>
> • If there are still fingerprints or stains, use a special lens paper
> dipped in a small amount of lens cleaning fluid (do not spray directly
> on the front cover plate).
>
> • Wipe gently in a circular motion, avoid back and forth wiping, and
> check for residues after cleaning.

2.  Equipment surface: Wipe the body with a soft dry cloth to remove
    > dust and stains. Avoid using organic solvents such as alcohol.

3.  Light source system: Turn off the light source and clean the heat
    > dissipation holes to ensure good ventilation.

### Usage precautions

4.  Avoid collision: Handle the equipment with care when moving to
    > prevent accuracy from being affected by vibration.

5.  Mark protection: Avoid contamination or wear of specimen marks that
    > may affect measurement accuracy.

## 6.2. Periodic Maintenance

### Monthly maintenance

6.  Comprehensive cleaning:

> • Thoroughly clean and remove dust in gaps with a soft brush.
>
> • Check all connecting wires for wear or poor contact.
>
> • Clean the workbench to ensure no metal debris and other impurities.

### Half-year maintenance

1.  Calibration check: Test and verify the measurement accuracy with an
    > extensometer calibrator or calibration plate (the error should be
    > controlled within ±0.5%).

2.  Light source check: Check whether the light source brightness is
    > uniform, replace the aging light source if necessary.

## 6.3. Regular Calibration

1.  Calibration cycle: 12 months under normal use conditions (maximum
    > not exceeding 18 months). Shorten to 6 months when used frequently
    > or in harsh environments.

2.  Calibration conditions: Must be carried out at a temperature of
    > 20℃±2℃, humidity ≤70%, and vibration-free environment. The
    > temperature fluctuation during calibration shall not exceed 1℃/h.

3.  Calibration method:

> • Correctly install the equipment with the calibration device (such as
> extensometer calibrator).
>
> • Perform multi-level displacement calibration (such as standard
> displacements of 3mm, 5mm, 10mm, etc.).
>
> • Record the error between the measured value and the standard value
> to ensure it is within the equipment accuracy range (usually ±0.5% or
> ±1.5μm).
>
> • Save the record after calibration for traceability.
>
> • Note: The equipment shall be recalibrated immediately after repair,
> replacement of important components, or when there is doubt about the
> measurement accuracy.

## 6.4. Long-term Storage and Special Case Handling

### Long-term storage (more than 4 weeks)

1.  Thoroughly clean and dry the equipment.

2.  Place the equipment in the original shockproof box or special
    > instrument cabinet with desiccant inside.

3.  Keep the storage environment temperature at 5℃~35℃, relative
    > humidity ≤60%, without corrosive gas.

4.  Check once a quarter to ensure the desiccant is effective and the
    > equipment is free from mildew or damage.

### Special case handling

1.  Lens mildew: Contact professional maintenance personnel immediately
    > if slight mildew is found on the lens. Do not wipe it by yourself.

2.  Equipment water ingress / moisture: Cut off the power immediately,
    > do not turn on the machine. Place it in a dry environment to air
    > dry naturally before sending for repair.

3.  Accuracy abnormality: When measurement results show deviations,
    > first check:

> • Whether the lens is clean
>
> • Whether the marks are clear
>
> • Whether the environment meets the requirements
>
> • If all are normal, calibration should be performed or contact our
> company's technical support.
